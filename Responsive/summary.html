Random Information

  • Passengers & Crew: The Titanic had about 2,224 people on board, including 1,317 passengers and 907 crew members.
  • Lifeboats: The ship had 20 lifeboats, enough for only 1,178 people, even though the total capacity was over 3,300.
  • Size: The Titanic was 882 feet 9 inches (269 meters) long and 92 feet 6 inches (28 meters) wide.
  • Cost: The total cost to build the Titanic was about $7.5 million in 1912, equivalent to hundreds of millions today.

Famous Passengers, The Captain, and The Discovery

  • Famous Passengers: Some well-known passengers included John Jacob Astor IV (one of the richest men in the world), Isidor and Ida Straus (owners of Macy’s), and Margaret "Molly" Brown (who later became famous as "The Unsinkable Molly Brown").
  • Captain: The ship’s captain was Edward Smith, who went down with the ship.
  • Discovery: The Titanic wreck was discovered in 1985 by Robert Ballard, about 12,500 feet (3,800 meters) below the North Atlantic Ocean.

Movies Made About The Titanic

Titanic (1997)

  • Director: James Cameron
  • Starring: Leonardo DiCaprio, Kate Winslet, Billy Zane
  • Plot: A fictional love story between Jack and Rose set against the real-life sinking of the Titanic.
  • Notable acts include winning 11 Academy Awards, including Best Picture, and it was the highest-grossing movie of all time until Avatar (2009).

A Night to Remember (1958)

  • Director: Roy Ward Baker
  • Starring: Kenneth More, Ronald Allen, Robert Ayres
  • Plot: A detailed and realistic depiction of the Titanic disaster, based on Walter Lord’s book of the same name.

The Unsinkable Molly Brown (1964)

  • Director: Charles Walters
  • Starring: Debbie Reynolds, Harve Presnell
  • Plot: A musical biography of Margaret "Molly" Brown, a real-life Titanic survivor.
